Overview

The Zambian tertiary education system comprises universities, technical colleges, and vocational training institutions. Governed by the Ministry of Higher Education, it aims to provide high-quality education to support national development. Zambia has both public and private universities, with the University of Zambia (UNZA) and the Copperbelt University (CBU) being the most prominent public institutions. The curriculum focuses on diverse fields including sciences, humanities, and technical studies to meet the demands of various sectors. Despite significant advancements, the system faces challenges such as limited funding, inadequate infrastructure, and a shortage of qualified lecturers. Efforts are ongoing to address these issues through policy reforms and increased investment. The Zambian government also promotes international collaboration and student exchange programs to enhance educational standards and global competitiveness. However, access to tertiary education remains a challenge for many due to financial constraints. Overall, the Zambian tertiary education system is evolving, with a focus on improving quality, accessibility, and alignment with the countryâ€™s socio-economic needs.

Notes

Zambian universities offer a wide range of faculties and schools designed to cater to various academic and professional disciplines. Major public universities such as the University of Zambia (UNZA) and Copperbelt University (CBU) house several faculties, including the Faculty of Medicine, Faculty of Engineering, Faculty of Natural Sciences, Faculty of Education, and Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences.These faculties are further divided into specialized schools and departments. For instance, the School of Medicine offers programs in clinical medicine, public health, and biomedical sciences, while the School of Engineering covers civil, electrical, and mechanical engineering disciplines. Similarly, the Faculty of Natural Sciences includes schools for mathematics, chemistry, and biology. Private universities also contribute significantly, providing faculties in business, information technology, and law. These institutions emphasize practical skills and research, aiming to produce graduates who can contribute effectively to the national economy. Despite the diverse offerings, challenges such as limited resources, outdated infrastructure, and insufficient academic staff persist. Ongoing efforts focus on improving quality through curriculum updates, faculty development programs, and international collaborations. Overall, Zambian universities strive to offer comprehensive education across various faculties to meet the evolving needs of the nation.

Top Universities in Zambia and Their Programs

University of Zambia (UNZA)

  • School of Medicine: Medicine and Surgery, Nursing, Public Health
  • School of Engineering: Civil Engineering, Electrical Engineering, Mechanical Engineering
  • School of Education: Primary Education, Secondary Education, Educational Administration
  • School of Humanities and Social Sciences: Economics, Sociology, Development Studies

Copperbelt University (CBU)

  • School of Business: Business Administration, Accountancy, Marketing
  • School of Mines and Mineral Sciences: Mining Engineering, Metallurgy, Environmental Engineering
  • School of Built Environment: Architecture, Urban and Regional Planning, Quantity Surveying
  • School of Natural Resources: Forestry, Wildlife Management, Fisheries and Aquatic Sciences

Mulungushi University

  • School of Agriculture and Natural Resources: Agricultural Sciences, Agribusiness Management, Environmental Studies
  • School of Science, Engineering, and Technology: Computer Science, Information Technology, Engineering
  • School of Social Sciences: Psychology, International Relations, Public Administration

Zambia Catholic University

  • Faculty of Business Studies: Economics, Business Administration, Human Resource Management
  • Faculty of Education: Secondary Education, Educational Leadership, Curriculum Studies
